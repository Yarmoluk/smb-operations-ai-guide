    What's Different About Patrick's Problems¶

    Scott's emails were about specific deals and market intelligence — concrete pumps, HaaS models. Those compress well because the analysis is: "here's an industry, here's a product, here's a customer, map the variables."

    Patrick's email is harder. He described four distinct operational domains, each with different stakeholders, different data flows, different regulatory constraints, and different optimization targets:

    1. HR benefits optimization — IRS rules, pre-tax elections, employee-specific variables, annual enrollment cycles
    2. Bookkeeping & tax strategy — Wave Accounting gaps, forecasting, CapEx vs OpEx, changing IRS regulations
    3. Order management — Susan's 2-3 hour weekly ritual of email triage, portal logins, spreadsheet updates, Monday meeting prep
    4. Hospital staffing — 3 shifts, variable demand, skill/certification matching, agency vs captive staff, real-time rebalancing

    Each of these is a different AI application pattern. The fact that one email surfaces all four — and that a structured analysis of each can be published in minutes — is the point. This isn't one problem. It's a portfolio of automation opportunities, each decomposed into its variables, constraints, and solution architecture.

    Patrick identified the problems. This guide maps the solutions.
